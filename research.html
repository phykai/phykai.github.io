Research

Our group studies non-Hermitian physics, open quantum systems, and non-equilibrium quantum matter. We are particularly interested in how dissipation, gain and loss, non-reciprocity, and measurement-induced processes give rise to new forms of topology, dynamics, localization, and many-body phenomena.

Non-Hermitian Band Theory and Skin Effects

A central direction of our research is the development of non-Bloch band theory for non-Hermitian systems. We investigate how point-gap topology, spectral winding, and generalized Brillouin zones determine the emergence of the non-Hermitian skin effect under open boundary conditions.

Representative works include:

  • Correspondence between winding numbers and skin modes in non-Hermitian systems, Physical Review Letters 125, 126402 (2020).
  • Non-Hermitian bulk-boundary correspondence and auxiliary generalized Brillouin zone theory, Physical Review Letters 125, 226402 (2020).

Geometry-Dependent and Higher-Dimensional Skin Effects

Beyond one dimension, non-Hermitian skin modes can depend sensitively on the sample geometry. We study universal aspects of higher-dimensional skin effects, spectral moment constraints, and generalized Brillouin-zone structures in two and higher dimensions.

Representative works include:

  • Universal non-Hermitian skin effect in two and higher dimensions, Nature Communications 13, 2496 (2022).

Algebraic Non-Hermitian Skin Effect

We also study quasi-long-range non-Hermitian skin modes, where the localization becomes algebraic rather than exponential. This leads to a generalized Fermi-surface description of higher-dimensional non-Hermitian systems.

Representative works include:

  • Algebraic non-Hermitian skin effect and generalized Fermi surface formula in arbitrary dimensions, Phys. Rev. X 15, 031039 (2025).

Open Quantum Systems and Non-Equilibrium Dynamics

More broadly, we are interested in many-body dynamics in open quantum systems, including the effects of dissipation, decoherence, and gain–loss processes on correlated and non-equilibrium quantum matter.
