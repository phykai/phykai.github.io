Research

Our group studies non-Hermitian physics, open quantum systems, and non-equilibrium quantum matter. We are particularly interested in how dissipation, gain and loss, non-reciprocity, and measurement-induced processes give rise to new forms of topology, dynamics, localization, and many-body phenomena.

Non-Hermitian Band Theory and Skin Effects

A central direction of our research is the development of non-Bloch band theory for non-Hermitian systems. We investigate how point-gap topology, spectral winding, and generalized Brillouin zones determine the emergence of the non-Hermitian skin effect under open boundary conditions.

Representative works include:

  • Correspondence between winding numbers and skin modes in non-Hermitian systems, Physical Review Letters 125, 126402 (2020).
  • Non-Hermitian bulk-boundary correspondence and auxiliary generalized Brillouin zone theory, Physical Review Letters 125, 226402 (2020).

Geometry-Dependent and Higher-Dimensional Skin Effects

Beyond one dimension, non-Hermitian skin modes can depend sensitively on the sample geometry. We study universal aspects of higher-dimensional skin effects, spectral moment constraints, and generalized Brillouin-zone structures in two and higher dimensions.

Representative works include:

  • Universal non-Hermitian skin effect in two and higher dimensions, Nature Communications 13, 2496 (2022).
  • Universal spectral moment theorem and its applications in non-Hermitian systems, Physical Review Letters 133, 216401 (2024).
  • Two-dimensional asymptotic generalized Brillouin zone conjecture, arXiv:2311.16868.

Algebraic Non-Hermitian Skin Effect

Another direction concerns quasi-long-range non-Hermitian skin modes. In contrast to conventional exponentially localized skin modes, algebraic skin modes exhibit power-law localization and require a broader non-Bloch framework applicable in arbitrary dimensions.

Representative works include:

  • Algebraic non-Hermitian skin effect and unified non-Bloch band theory in arbitrary dimensions, accepted by Physical Review X.
  • Edge theory of the non-Hermitian skin modes in higher dimensions, Physical Review B 109, 165127 (2024).
  • Ultra spectral sensitivity and non-local bi-impurity bound states from quasi-long-range non-Hermitian skin modes, arXiv:2409.13623.

Non-Hermitian Dynamics and Wavepacket Propagation

We study dynamical consequences of non-Hermitian band structures, including directional invisibility, dynamical degeneracy splitting, anisotropic Bloch oscillations, and experimental signatures of geometry-dependent skin effects.

Representative works include:

  • Dynamical degeneracy splitting and directional invisibility in non-Hermitian systems, Physical Review Letters 131, 036402 (2023).
  • Geometry-dependent skin effect and anisotropic Bloch oscillations in a non-Hermitian optical lattice, Physical Review A 109, 023317 (2024).
  • Observation of the geometry-dependent skin effect and dynamical degeneracy splitting, Science Bulletin (2023).

Impurity and Bound-State Physics in Non-Reciprocal Systems

We also investigate impurity-induced bound states in non-Hermitian systems. These systems exhibit unusual point-gap bound states, non-local impurity responses, and bound-state geometries that have no direct analogue in Hermitian systems.

Representative works include:

  • Point-gap bound states in non-Hermitian systems, Physical Review B 108, 165132 (2023).
  • Tailoring bound state geometry in high-dimensional non-Hermitian systems, arXiv:2406.07626.
  • Algebraic states in continuum in d > 1 dimensional non-Hermitian systems, arXiv:2507.06514.

Open Quantum Systems and Non-Equilibrium Dynamics

Since non-Hermitian physics naturally arises in open systems, we are interested in the relation between effective non-Hermitian dynamics and full quantum master-equation dynamics. Topics include dissipative transport, periodically driven open systems, dynamical critical behavior, and mixed-state quantum phases.

Representative works include:

  • Loss-induced universal one-way transport in periodically driven systems, Physical Review B 109, 184302 (2024).
  • Helical damping and dynamical critical skin effect in open quantum systems, Physical Review Research 2, 043167 (2020).

Exceptional Points and Non-Hermitian Degeneracies

We study exceptional points and non-Hermitian degeneracies in both few-level systems and lattice models. These degeneracies control anisotropic responses, spectral singularities, and non-Bloch band structures.

Representative works include:

  • Polariton-driven reentry and anisotropic exceptional points in a PT-symmetric ternary system, Physical Review A 109, 053503 (2024).
  • Non-Bloch Dirac points and phase diagram in the stacked non-Hermitian SSH model, Physical Review B 111, L241109 (2025).
